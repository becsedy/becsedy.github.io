More About My Trip to Iceland

I had previously been to Europe a couple times with my family: Italy in 2022, and England and France in 2023, but this was my first time co-ordinating a trip with my friends to the continent (although I'm unsure whether calling Iceland "the continent" would be correct). As a vegetarian and a lover of exploring cities and nature, two things that were really important to me during the planning phase of this trip were to find delicious restaurants with vegetarian offerings, and to find fun activities for my friends and I to do to get immersed in both Icelandic culture and Icelandic natural beauty.

Picking the trips were relatively easy to do, since we all had a similar budget of what we were willing to spend on the tripa nd also had a good idea of what we would all want to do together, but finding restaurants in the vicinity of our AirBnb that were relatively cheap while also accommodating to all of our dietary needs and preferences was no easy task. One of our friends doesn't like vegetables, another disliked seafood, I'm a vegetarian, another friend was basically vegetarian, so it was tough to decide consistently on places to eat where all seven of us would enjoy. Despite this, we found many places that served really good food that satisfied all of us.

Reykjavik Restaurant Recommendations

  • Deig Cafe - coffee, pastries, bagels
  • Wake n Bake - coffee, bagels
  • Viet Express - Chinese/Vietnamese food
  • 2 Guys - burgers, fries, American food
  • Public House Gastropub - Japanese-inspired pub food
  • Monako - bar

Activity Recommendations

  • Perlan Museum
  • Lava Cave Tour
  • Golden Circle Tour
  • Northern Lights Tour (when it's not cloudy)
  • Sky Lagoon (highly recommended)
