My Professional Life

I am a fourth-year honors undergraduate student from Southbury, CT studying mathematics, physics, and data science at Northeastern University. From an early age, I have been fascinated by the power of data and its potential to solve complex problems, and this fascination has led me to take on academic and career experience in all three subjects, obtained by taking classes and working on several projects.

Through these projects, I have contributed to the design of management software for a small rocket payload system, developed a prototype application in AppSmith for a hospital database management system, created an interactive travel interface for analyzing the characteristics of different travel itineraries, and partnered with InnSure Corporation to analyze data regarding employees of 800+ insurance companies to mitigate social engineering and cyber-attacks on these companies.

My professional journey has further honed my skills and fueled my interest in applying data-driven approaches to real-world challenges. At Finwave Semiconductor, I developed a comprehensive library of statistical and numeric methods for analyzing and visualizing measurement data, which significantly enhanced the efficiency of research and design processes. I also consulted existing engineering literature to create a predictive numerical model for the thermal behavior of semiconductor devices, achieving results with high accuracy.

Additionally, I have work experience as an administrative assistant, where I designed and implemented a logistical storage system for a manufacturing company, and as a research assistant under a Northeastern University professor, where I assisted in the compilation of a database of grant and funding information for non-profit organizations.

I love working on all things technical and data-based and I take pride in my work ethic, my continual strive to learn, and my curiosity about how the world works.
